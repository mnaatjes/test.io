# What Is Phishing? Notes

## Definition
Social engineering involving counterfeit emails, messengers, and websites designed to mimic legitimate entities (banks, gov agencies).

## Target Information
- Social security numbers.
- Bank/credit card info.
- Online passwords.

## Technical Threats
- **Reverse-Connected Shellcode:** Encoded in attachments; bypasses antivirus to connect back to the attacker.
- **Firewall Limitations:** Security mechanisms may allow these connections.

## Suspicious Email Checklist
- Do you know the sender?
- Expected message/attachment?
- Header/Subject/Attachment sense?
- Strange characters?
- Spelling/Grammar errors?
- Virus flag from updated antivirus?

## Prohibited Actions (DON'Ts)
- Do not click links (e.g., bank account update requests).
- Do not open attachments/run macros from unknown senders.
- Do not respond to or forward suspicious emails.
- Do not share Gov ID info.

## Required Actions (DOs)
- **Report Phish:** Use the "Check SPAM/Malicious" or "Report Phish" button in Outlook.
- Delete malicious emails only after reporting.

## Top 7 Phishing Signs
1. Unfamiliar/illegitimate address.
2. Sense of urgency ("Urgent!", "Action Required!").
3. Generic greeting ("Dear Customer").
4. Spelling/Grammar mistakes.
5. Uncommon requests (money/sensitive data).
6. Suspicious links (hover to verify destination).
7. Suspicious attachments (.exe, unusual types).
