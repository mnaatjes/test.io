# Social Engineering Techniques Notes

## Core Concept
Non-technical intrusion relying on human interaction/manipulation to bypass security. Exploits the human tendency to trust.

## Techniques
- **Simple Direct Request:** Simply asking for info.
- **Dumpster Diving:** Searching trash for identity-stealing data.
- **Raiding Mailboxes:** Illegally stealing postal mail.
- **Phishing:** Counterfeit emails/sites to deceive users.
- **Impersonation:** Pretending to be authority figures (IT support, managers).
- **Surfing Company Websites:** Gathering public data (employee info, financials).

## Intruder's Toolkit
- **Vulnerability Scanning:** "Pre-attack probe" for weaknesses.
- **Password Cracker:** Automated guessing/dictionary attacks.
- **Network Spoofing:** Impersonating sign-on to steal passwords.
- **Viruses:** Programs that infect files by self-copying.
- **Worms:** Independent programs reproducing across networks (often email).
- **Logic Bombs:** Code triggered by specific conditions.
- **Ransomware:** Encrypts files for ransom (Bitcoin).
- **DoS Attacks:** Overloading machines/networks to cause unavailability.

## Social Engineering Defense
- **Weakest Link:** Human factor (phishing, weak passwords, ignoring updates).
- **Tester Responsibility:** Safeguard Test IO/customer info.
- **Vigilance Actions:**
  - Maintain strong security controls on servers/desktops.
  - Use firewalls.
  - Keep resources updated with patches/upgrades.
